Mission & Vision

Mission: To improve the well‑being of members of the Queer community in Rwanda through skill‑building, healthcare support, and sustainable volunteer programs.

Vision: A healthy, careful community in which human rights are completely promoted — irrespective of sexual orientation — and every member contributes to positive change in perception.

Our Goals & Core Values

Goals:

  • Enhance skills and knowledge through training and workshops.
  • Provide healthcare services that promote health and care for members and beneficiaries.
  • Support anyone wishing to work with the marginalized community via a sustainable volunteer program.

Core Values: Equality, Stigma‑free Support, Community‑led Advocacy, Confidentiality, Inclusivity.

Our Journey — Annual Milestones

2019 — Foundation

HRI was officially registered in Rwanda, born from community‑led activism to fill a critical gap in rights awareness and support for marginalized adults.

2020 — First Workshops & COVID‑19 Response

Despite the pandemic, we pivoted to digital learning and small‑group sessions, reaching 120 adults with legal literacy and stigma‑reduction resources.

2021 — Helpline & Safe Spaces Launched

We introduced a confidential helpline and safe‑space programme, providing over 200 counselling sessions and referrals to legal/medical support.

2022 — Policy Dialogue & First District Report

Facilitated 40+ policy dialogues with local authorities and submitted our first evidence‑based policy recommendation report.

2023 — Community Advocate Network

Trained and certified 200+ community advocates across all provinces; anti‑stigma campaigns reached 5,000+ individuals.

2024 — Expanding Reach & Measuring Impact

Over 300 adults trained in legal literacy (84% confidence increase), 150+ policy dialogues held, and a 97% recommendation rate from beneficiaries.

2025 & Beyond — Deepening Systemic Change

Target: expand to all 5 provinces, grow advocate network to 500+, launch digital rights‑tracking tool, and institutionalize inclusive governance engagements.

Future Targets

  • Reach 2,500 adults with rights education by 2027.
  • Expand safe‑space centres to two additional provinces.
  • Launch a digital legal‑aid platform for remote communities.
  • Influence at least 3 district‑level policies that explicitly protect marginalized and vulnerable communities' rights.
  • Build a sustainable volunteer corps of 500 trained advocates.
