Who We Are

About HRI Rwanda

Health and Rights Rwanda (HRI) is a grassroots civil society organisation founded in Rwanda with a bold mission: to ensure every adult — regardless of sexual orientation, gender identity, or background — lives free from stigma, discrimination, and human rights violations.

We were born from the community, shaped by lived experience, and driven by an unwavering belief that dignity and equality are not privileges — they are rights.

🎯 Our Mission

To stand with every community member facing human rights violations or mistreatment, with a focus on sexual orientation and stigma reduction.

🔭 Our Vision

A Rwanda where every adult lives with dignity, equality, and freedom — recognised and protected.

Core Values

⚖️ Equality 💚 Stigma-free Support 🤲 Community-led Advocacy 🔒 Confidentiality 🌍 Inclusivity
Our Work

What We Do

From legal literacy to safe spaces, HRI offers holistic support for those whose rights have been denied or undermined.

Rights Awareness

We conduct workshops and legal literacy sessions for adults across Rwanda — helping people understand their rights, know the law, and speak up against violations. Special focus is given to communities facing stigma based on sexual orientation.

Learn more

Support Services

Our support team offers counselling, safe spaces, and referral pathways for individuals who have experienced human rights violations, discrimination, or stigma. All services are confidential and stigma-free.

Get support

Advocacy & Policy

We run anti-stigma campaigns and engage directly with local authorities and policymakers to advance protections for all community members. Through evidence-based dialogue, we push for systemic change in Rwanda.
