Who We Are

About HRI Rwanda

Health and Rights Rwanda (HRI) is a grassroots civil society organisation founded in Rwanda with a bold mission: to ensure every adult — regardless of sexual orientation, gender identity, or background — lives free from stigma, discrimination, and human rights violations.

We were born from the community, shaped by lived experience, and driven by an unwavering belief that dignity and equality are not privileges — they are rights.

🎯 Our Mission

HRI's mission is to improve the well-being of the members of the LGBTI community in Rwanda.

🔭 Our Vision

The organization's vision is to be a careful and healthy community in which human rights are totally promoted with respect to LGBTI members and in which all members of the community contribute to positive change on the perception of Rwandan LGBTI people irrespective of their sexual orientation differentiation.

Our Goals

• To improve the skills and knowledge of the members of the LGBTI community in Rwanda through trainings and workshops

• To contribute to the healthcare system by providing services related to the promotion of health and care of members and beneficiaries of HRIRWANDA.

• To support whoever wishing to work with the members of the LGBTI community in Rwanda throughout a sustainable volunteer program

Core Values

⚖️ Equality 💚 Stigma-free Support 🤲 Community-led Advocacy 🔒 Confidentiality 🌍 Inclusivity

Rights Awareness

Legal Literacy Workshops for Everyone

HRI conducts comprehensive legal literacy workshops designed to empower adults across Rwanda with knowledge of their fundamental rights and the laws that protect them.

What We Teach

  • Rights Fundamentals: Understanding your constitutional and human rights
  • Anti-Discrimination Laws: Protections against discrimination based on various grounds
  • Reporting Mechanisms: How to report violations to appropriate authorities
  • Legal Resources: Access to legal aid and support services
  • Advocacy Skills: How to speak up and claim your rights

Our Approach

We focus on interactive, community-based learning using local languages (Kinyarwanda, French, English). Our workshops are designed to be accessible to all education levels and are FREE for participants.

Special Focus

We prioritize reaching communities facing stigma and discrimination, including those based on sexual orientation and gender identity. Every participant is guaranteed a safe, stigma-free learning environment.

Recent Impact

In 2024, we reached over 300 adults across Rwanda through our rights awareness initiatives, with 84% reporting increased confidence in claiming their rights after participation.

Support Services

Confidential & Stigma-Free Support

HRI provides comprehensive support services for individuals who have experienced human rights violations, discrimination, or stigma. All services are strictly confidential and delivered without judgment.

Services We Offer

  • Counselling: One-on-one emotional support from trained counsellors
  • Safe Spaces: Group support sessions in confidential settings
  • Legal Referrals: Connection to legal professionals and advocacy networks
  • Medical Referrals: Support in accessing healthcare services
  • Crisis Support: Immediate assistance for those in acute distress
  • Follow-up Support: Long-term accompaniment through recovery

How We Work

Our support team includes trained volunteers and professional counsellors. We use a person-centered approach, respecting your autonomy and cultural background. You decide the pace and nature of support you receive.

Confidentiality Guarantee

Your privacy is sacred to us. All information shared is protected. We will never share your details, experiences, or identity without your explicit written consent. The only exception is if there is immediate danger to life.

Advocacy & Policy

Driving Systemic Change in Rwanda

HRI campaigns for policy changes and engages directly with government and community leaders to create lasting protections for all, especially those facing discrimination based on sexual orientation.

Our Advocacy Focus

  • Anti-Stigma Campaigns: Community awareness initiatives to reduce discrimination
  • Policy Dialogue: Structured engagement with government officials and lawmakers
  • Evidence-Based Advocacy: Using research and data to support our recommendations
  • Community Leadership: Amplifying the voices of those directly affected
  • Capacity Building: Training community advocates to champion change

Recent Advocacy Wins

  • 150+ policy dialogues held with local authorities (2023-2024)
  • Anti-stigma campaigns reaching over 5,000 community members
  • Engagement with district officials on inclusive local governance
  • Training of 200+ community advocates

Our Strategy

We believe change happens when communities lead. Our advocacy is informed by lived experience of those facing violations. We work collaboratively with allies across civil society, government, and international partners.

March for Equality 2025 — A historic and vibrant march in Kigali, Rwanda, bringing together over 200 community members, rights defenders, and allies united in advocacy for human rights, dignity, and equality for all. Participants peacefully marched through the streets, carrying banners, chanting slogans, and raising awareness about LGBTQ+ rights, anti-stigma initiatives, and inclusive governance. These structured dialogues fostered meaningful conversations around human rights protections, non-discrimination frameworks, and community-centered policy development. 1 of 6

Community Rights Workshop — Legal Literacy Training (July 2024) — Interactive and empowering legal literacy training sessions held across the Eastern Province, reaching 80+ adults from rural and urban communities. Participants learned about constitutional rights, anti-discrimination laws, reporting mechanisms, and practical advocacy strategies. Facilitators used local languages (Kinyarwanda, French, English) and community-based methods to ensure accessibility. All participants received certificates and continued support resources.
